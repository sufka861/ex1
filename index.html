Enjoying black Turkish cofee

In Turkey, when you go to somebody's house, the first question isn't if you want to drink Turkish coffee, but rather how you would like to have your coffee prepared. To answer the question, you may say "sade" which means no sugar; "az seker" which means very little sugar; "orta" which means with 1-2 teaspoons of sugar; or "sekerli" which means with 3-4 teaspoon sugar.

Ingredients:

  • Cold filtered water
  • Turkish coffee: Turkish coffee is much more finely ground than regular coffee
  • Cezve (a special wide-bottomed pot, usually made of copper)
  • Granulated sugar
  • Turkish coffee cups

How to:

  1. Fill the pot 3/4 full with cold filtered water (measure how many cups of water fit into the 3/4 line)
  2. Boil the water over a medium flame
  3. Add 1 full cized teaspoon for each cup of water
  4. Let the pot boil once more with the cofee inside
  5. Turn the flame of, tap the pot 3 times on a hard counter, and let it sit for a minute
  6. Pour the cofee into the cups, make sure to stop before pouring the grains
  7. Sit back, and enjoy
